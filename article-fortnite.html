Fortnite's Festival Mode Now Supports Microphones and Drums

April 15, 2026 · 3 min read

🎸

Epic Games just dropped a major update to Fortnite Festival — the Rock Band-like music mode built into Fortnite. As of Thursday, you can now use real microphones to sing and connect Rock Band 4 or compatible MIDI drum kits to play along. Yes, that means your old Rock Band equipment from 15 years ago might still work.

What's New

Fortnite Festival already let you play guitar controllers. Now the full band experience is here: any microphone that works with Fortnite on PC or console for singing, and Rock Band 4 drum kits (or compatible MIDI setups) for drumming. The mode already had guitar, bass, and keyboard parts, so this fills out the band.

The update launches Thursday and also features Laufey — an Icelandic-Chinese artist — as the new headline performer for Season 14.

Why It's a Big Deal

✓ Gaming meets physical music: This bridges the gap between passive gaming and actually playing an instrument. For people who grew up with Rock Band and Guitar Hero but don't play anymore, this could be a reason to pick up the plastic guitars again. And for kids who've never tried it, it's a low-barrier entry point to music gaming.

Why Some Are Skeptical

✗ Is this a gimmick? Most people aren't going to set up a MIDI drum kit just to play Fortnite. Microphone support for singing sounds fun in theory, but in practice it could be chaos. And there's a question of how well the game detects pitch and timing — which is what made the original Rock Band addictive versus just "noisy fun."

The Broader Trend

Epic has been quietly turning Fortnite into more than just a battle royale. There's Creative (user-made game modes), concert events, and now a full music platform layered on top. Whether that's exciting or exhausting probably depends on how you feel about Fortnite as a platform.
