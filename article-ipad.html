iPad Air Rumored to Get OLED Display in 2027 — Is It Worth Waiting For?

April 15, 2026 · 2 min read

📱

Apple added OLED to the iPad Pro back in 2024, and now it looks like the iPad Air is next. According to a report from ET News citing Apple Insider, the Air could get an OLED upgrade in the first half of 2027 — though a conflicting Bloomberg report suggested it would come after an OLED iPad Mini in 2026. So... maybe 2027.

What OLED Actually Gives You

OLED screens have each pixel emit its own light, meaning black pixels turn completely off — true black, infinite contrast, and colors that pop. Compared to LCD, the difference is immediately noticeable, especially watching movies or viewing photos. OLED also uses slightly less power for dark content since pixels can be fully off.

Buy Now or Wait?

✓ Wait if you can: If you're not in a rush, an OLED iPad Air in 2027 could be a significant jump in display quality. If you watch a lot of video, edit photos, or draw, it might be worth holding off. A lot can happen in a year, and OLED prices may come down further.

✗ Buy now if you need it now: The current iPad Air with M-series chips is already incredibly fast. Unless you're doing color-accurate creative work where OLED would matter, the difference might not be worth the wait. Technology moves fast, and the "best time to buy" is always relative to your actual needs right now.

The Timeline Question

Apple's roadmap leaks are notoriously unreliable. ET News has a mixed track record. Bloomberg's Mark Gurman is generally more reliable but has also gotten timing wrong. The honest answer: OLED is coming to more iPads. When exactly for the Air? Probably 2027, but maybe not.
