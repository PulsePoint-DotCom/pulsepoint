Laufey Is Headlining Fortnite Festival — Here's Why That Matters

April 15, 2026 · 3 min read

🎤

Icelandic-Chinese artist Laufey is the new headline act for Fortnite Festival Season 14 — and if you're not familiar with her, now's a good time to catch up. She's been quietly building one of the most interesting careers in modern music, blending classical training with pop sensibility in a way that's hard to categorize — and that's kind of the point.

Who Is Laufey?

Laufey (pronounced LAY-vay) is a 24-year-old artist from Reykjavik, Iceland, with Chinese heritage. She started playing cello at age 4, studied at the Icelandic Conservatory, and later moved to the US to study at Berklee College of Music. Her music sits somewhere between jazz, pop, and classical. Her 2023 album "Everything I Dream About" landed on Billboard Classical and Jazz charts simultaneously.

Why Fortnite Is a Big Deal for Artists

✓ Fortnite as a launchpad: Epic Games has turned Fortnite into a music platform in a way no other game has. Travis Scott's 2020 concert had 12 million concurrent viewers. For an artist like Laufey — talented but not yet a household name — being the face of a Fortnite season is exposure that most traditional promotion can't buy.

The Other Side

✗ Is this what music has become? Some argue that artists performing in video games is a symptom of how streaming devalued music itself. When you can't make a living off album sales alone, performing as a digital avatar in a battle royale starts to look like the best option.

What You Need to Know

Season 14 of Fortnite Festival drops Thursday. If you play Fortnite, you'll be able to access Laufey's music and exclusive in-game items. If you don't, it's still worth looking her up on Spotify — her stuff is genuinely good.
