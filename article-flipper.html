Flipper Zero Can Rickroll Electronic Price Tags — Here's Why

April 15, 2026 · 4 min read

📡

The Flipper Zero — a small hacker gadget that's part Tamagotchi, part Swiss Army knife for wireless protocols — has picked up a new trick. Using a tool called TagTinker, the device can now rewrite electronic shelf price tags in stores. Yes, someone already got it to display "Rickroll" lyrics instead of the actual price. Because of course it happened.

What Is a Flipper Zero?

It's a $169 handheld device that can read, record, and emulate wireless signals — RFID, NFC, Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, infrared, and more. It's marketed as a tool for security researchers and hackers-to-learn, but it's gained a reputation for being used for pranks and "white hat" mischief. The design is deliberately playful — it looks like a Tamagotchi crossed with a Game Boy.

What TagTinker Does

TagTinker lets the Flipper Zero communicate with electronic price tags — the digital displays you see in stores showing product names and prices. These tags use wireless protocols to receive updates from a central system. TagTinker essentially lets you inject custom content into those tags, with the creators calling it "for educational research and testing" and noting that testing at a real store "is strictly prohibited."

The Fun Argument

✓ It's a harmless prank: Nobody gets hurt by a price tag displaying "Rickroll" instead of $4.99. It's the digital equivalent of switching the "wet floor" sign to say "slip n slide." Security research that exposes real vulnerabilities is valuable.

The Serious Concern

✗ This is more than pranks: The same technique could theoretically be used to change prices at self-checkout. Retailers will need to actually secure these wireless protocols. And "don't test at stores" is a disclaimer nobody will follow.

The Bigger Picture

Most "smart" devices weren't designed with strong security. The Flipper Zero is just making visible what security researchers have been saying for years: the wireless world around you is far more hackable than you'd expect.
