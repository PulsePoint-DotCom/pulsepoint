Track Season Is Here — Athletes to Watch This Year

April 15, 2026 · 4 min read

Spring means one thing for high school sports fans: track season is officially underway. From regional qualifiers to state championships heating up in the coming months, here's everything you need to know about who's making noise right now.

The Standout Stories

Every year there are a few athletes who come out of nowhere and make you stop scrolling. This year is no different. A sophomore from Georgia just ran a 4:12 mile — a time that would've qualified for NCAA finals last season. She's already getting attention from major college programs.

On the boys' side, a junior from Texas broke the regional record in the 800m last weekend by nearly a full second. He's been consistent all season and coaches say his form is technically flawless — meaning there's likely more to come as he peaks for championship season.

XC to Track Transition

If you ran cross country this fall, you're in a good spot. The endurance you built carrying through the winter translates directly to track. The key events for XC runners moving up: the 1600m, 3200m, and distance relays. If you can hold pace over 5K on grass and hills, 1600m on a track should feel fast.

What to Watch For

Regional qualifiers are announced in the next few weeks. If your times are close to the cutoffs, now is the time to race smart — not all-out. Post-season races are won by athletes who peak at the right moment, not necessarily the fastest ones in April.
